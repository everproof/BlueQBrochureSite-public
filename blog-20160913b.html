Is your organisation Child Safe Ready?

Posted by Virginia Krantz on 13/09/2016

On 1 January 2017 the new Victorian compulsory Child Safe Standards come into effect for all organisations that deal with children.

As the deadline for the new Victorian Child Safe Standards has drawn closer, our services at BlueQ have grown as we have been inundated with requests from our Victorian clients asking us for advice on how we could further help them comply with the Standards. In response to these requests, we created  childsafeready.com.au - a short self-assessment and suite of free resources and tailored information to help organisations meet the Child Safe Standards.

Do the standards apply to you?

Victorian organisations that provide services to children will be required under the Child Safety and Wellbeing Act to ensure that they implement compulsory child safe standards to protect children from harm.

BlueQ helps organisations meet the Child Safe Standards, in particular Standard 4, by automatically verifying the validity of Working With Children Checks on a weekly basis and provides notifications of any negative result.

Without BlueQ, checking whether an individual has a valid Working with Children Check is a manual process. The name and card number of every staff member must be entered on the Victorian Department of Justice and Regulation’s Check Status Facility each time it needs to be checked. For organisations with a large number of employees, it can be a very time-consuming process to ensure all staff hold a valid Working with Children Check. Fortunately, BlueQ helps to make this process a breeze!

BlueQ is offering 15 minute consultations to help organisations become Child Safe Ready for 1st January 2017. To take self-assessment and book a 15-minute consultation visit childsafeready.com.au
